THE FUTURE OF ABBEY ARTS

WHO IS ABBEY ARTS?

Abbey Arts is an independent, non-religious, nonprofit providing creative events at unusual spaces around Seattle. This organization aims to create affordable, welcoming and inclusive experiences where people can learn and develop through education and performance in many different art mediums. Ultimately, Abbey Arts strives to be a community-driven, volunteer-powered organization, that hosts events that are made of a comfortable safe space where a wide variety of people from different income levels, skills, ethnicities, backgrounds can interact. MISSION STATEMENT ▼
Abbey Arts curates welcoming arts & cultural experiences for people of all ages and incomes to explore creativity, enjoy beauty, grow empathy and increase awareness.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

How might we enable Abbey Arts to achieve a robust, well-designed, and user-friendly website so that they can attract a larger audience and further engage the community?

INFORMATION PROBLEM ▼
Our information solution will aim to improve website design and seamless integrations for the current Abbey Arts website. For website design, we want to focus on branding, navigation, interactions, and content. Specifically, we want to conduct thorough primary and secondary UI/UX research to ensure that our proposed designs and approach to development reflects contemporary trends in how performing arts organizations advertise themselves via their websites. In doing so, we can ensure that users have a positive experience on Abbey Arts' website that will encourage them to stay on the site longer, make purchases, and return to Abbey Arts (both online and in-person) in the future.

For integrations, we want to focus on the G Suite and analytics. By providing this solution, users will be able to understand the purpose of information on the website and easily comprehend the events and programs possible through Abbey Arts, hopefully increasing website traffic and ticket sales as well. The way information on the site is presented to potential customers, performers, and facility renters is crucial to how they initially perceive Abbey Arts, particularly if the site is their first encounter they have with the organization.

PROJECT GOAL

To create a website for Abbey Arts, a local nonprofit arts organization looking to streamline the online experience of guests and potential renters, as well as boost their profile in the arts community. The site is outdated and needs to be modernized.

PROJECT BACKGROUND ▼
As a non-profit, Abbey Arts' limited resources prevent them from pursuing all projects, one of them being the creation of an effective and robust website. The absence of a modern, user-friendly, and well-designed website reduces their ability to attract more customers and performers. Currently, the outdated website is challenging to navigate, has no clear design scheme, and gives a sense of information overload. These aspects of website design and function are crucial to maintaining a customer base and creating a user-friendly experience. Additionally, the back-end processes are not streamlined which causes disconnect in internal business processes. Without a strong functioning website and clear communication with customers and attendees, Abbey Arts is unsuccessful in fulfilling their mission to engage and connect the Seattle community through live music and the arts.

DESIGN PROCESS

OUR DESIGN PROCESS

DEFINE

Our problem statement stated above clearly defines the problem we are trying to solve in a way that is specific, measurable, and achievable. It also take into account the needs and requirements of the users or stakeholders.

Our project goal defines the desired outcome of the project and provides a clear direction for the rest of the design process. It aligns with the problem statement and takes into account the resources, time, and budget available.

Our information problem has a clear understanding of the current state of knowledge about the problem and any gaps that need to be filled in order to solve it. It includes an analysis of the information needs of the users or stakeholders and an understanding of the sources and types of information that are available.

Overall, the Define stage was critical for setting the foundation for the rest of the design process and ensuring that the project is aligned with the needs of the users or stakeholders.

RESEARCH

Our insight research uncovered user needs, pain points, and behaviors for our project. It was essential to the initial stages of our project as it provided valuable insights that informed the design of user-centered products and services that needed to be addressed. To conduct this research, we held user interviews and usability tests throughout the entire design process to ensure that user needs are met.

Our user research helped gain insights into users' motivations, goals, and pain points so that we could make well-informed decisions about how to design the product so that it be both usable and enjoyable to use. One of our biggest learning experiences was attending an Abbey Arts event and getting to know our user group.

Our market research involved collecting and analyzing other similar existing websites, such as Neumos or The Crocadile Venues. The knowledge gained from this information was used to better understand the user needs and preferences, which further informed the development of the product and service. Additionally, it helped identify emerging trends and changes in user behavior, which likewise informed marketing and branding strategies.

Our literature review involved gathering and analyzing existing literature to gain insights into the current state of knowledge, identify gaps in the research, and synthesize findings.
